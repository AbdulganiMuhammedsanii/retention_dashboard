Methodology & Honest Caveats

Headline formula (your spec): Retention = (HC − Departures) / HC × 100 using start-of-period headcount. This is the SHRM standard "retention rate."

Annualized turnover = sum of departures over trailing 12 months ÷ average headcount across those months. This is the BLS / ASIS comparable metric.

12-month rolling retention = product of monthly retention rates over the period (compounded), which more accurately reflects sustained workforce stability than averaging.

⚠ Limitations of this formula: It treats voluntary resignations and involuntary terminations identically. For staffing decisions, distinguishing the two matters — losing a guard you fired is operationally different from losing a top performer to a competitor. Consider tracking departure reason as a future enhancement.

⚠ "HC" interpretation: Industry convention is start-of-period headcount (used here). Some firms use average HC, which produces slightly different numbers. Be consistent month-over-month either way.
